Planets of the Solar System
Planets are massive objects whose sheer sizes create gravitational forces for smaller objects to orbit around them
There are two types of planets in the solar system: Inner Planets and Outer Planets
Inner Planets
There are 4 inner planets in the solar system, they are called that because they are closest to the sun
  • Mercury, the smallest and closest planet to the Sun
  • Venus, the second closest planet to the Sun also known as Earth's sister planet
  • Earth, the blue planet and where all life in the Solar System is located
  • Mars, the red planet, and the farthest from the Sun in the inner planets
Outer Planets
The outer planets, also referred to as gas giants, are the farthest planets from the Sun
  • Jupiter, the biggest planet, famous for its big red spot
  • Saturn, known for its beautiful rings
  • Uranus, an ice planet
  • Neptune, the farthest from the sun
